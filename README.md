# Formal Elements

Players:
  Multiplayer game, atleast 2 players are required but any more than 2 also work <br />
  All players have the same role: steal a PC part until all items have been collected <br />
  The Player interactions are cooperative play <br />

Objective:
  Work as a team to steal 3 parts from the PC <br />

Serious Objective:
  Learn about computer architecture <br />

Proceedures:
  Starting Action:
    All players spawn in front of a gaming PC case <br />

  Progression of Action:
    Throughout the game, each player (thief) can move freely and try to steal a part of the PC. <br />
    Whenever they attempt to steal a part, they have to clear the enemies concealing information about the PC part. <br />
    After that, the PC part becomes unlocked for them to take and they can acquire the part. <br />

  Special Action:
    The players can / should interact with each other to come up with the right answer <br />

  Resolving Action:
    Whenever the enemies near a PC part have been cleared the players have acquired one item.  <br />
    When all enemies near all PC parts are correctly cleared and all items have been collected, the players are presented with one final question <br />
    If they answer the final question correctly, they win <br />

Rules: <br />
  Restrictive Action: <br />
    Can't access the PC parts before clearing the enemies nearby. <br />
    Can't win the game unless all three parts have acquired. <br />
    Questions are multiple choice, must select one right answer. <br />

Resources:
  Hints. Each time an enemy is cleared, you get a hint. The hints only appear for few seconds. <br />

Non-vanilla procedure / rule:
  The hints scattered across the stage should be collected as a team. <br />

Conflict:
  Choices. The team must choose the correct answer to the question. <br />

Boundries:
  The act of theft is only for the game, and is not encouraged in real life. <br />

Outcome:
  The team wins/loses <br />

Question:
  What does RAM stand for?

Answer:
  Random Access Memory
